Winter 2010 Project #9: MarkUs

19 Nov 2009, 15:58

MarkUs is an open-source tool which recreates the ease and flexibility of grading assignments with pen on paper, within a web application. It also allows students and instructors to form groups, and collaborate on assignments. MarkUs is implemented in Ruby on Rails, and every line of code has been written by undergraduate students. It is currently used in 3 classes at the University of Toronto. There are plans to use it for one course in Waterloo starting in January with the hopes of expanding our user base in the Fall of 2010.

Some Ideas for Next Term

16 Nov 2009, 19:17

Here are three suggestions for improving UCOSP next term [1]; comments would be very welcome. Have every mentoring project put together a list of 1 small task per week per student for the first four weeks of the project. The goal is to make sure that the mentors and the students are more organized and on the same page by the time the main work in the project starts. It will also serve as a weeding out period: students who can’t get four small tasks done are unlikely to be able to hack the rest of the course.
